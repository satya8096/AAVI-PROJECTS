Commercial and Industrial Electrician Services

Industrial Equipment Installation

Industrial electricians are responsible for installing, wiring, and commissioning heavy machinery and equipment used in manufacturing processes. This can include conveyor systems, motors, pumps, compressors, and control systems.

High-Voltage Services

Many industrial facilities operate high-voltage electrical systems. Electricians in this sector are trained to work with high-voltage components, including transformers, switchgear, and substations, while adhering to strict safety protocols.

Instrumentation and Calibration

Electricians in industrial settings work with various sensors and instruments to measure and control parameters like temperature, pressure, flow, and level. They calibrate these instruments to ensure accuracy.

Hazardous Area Electrical Work

In environments with flammable or explosive materials, electricians must follow specialized safety protocols and install equipment, like explosion-proof enclosures and conduit, to prevent ignition sources.

Power Quality Analysis

Electricians analyze the quality of electrical power in the facility, addressing issues like voltage sags, surges, and harmonics to ensure the proper functioning of equipment.

Process Control Systems

In industries like chemical manufacturing and food processing, electricians design, install, and maintain control systems that regulate and monitor various processes. This includes PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers) and SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition) systems.

Power Factor Correction

Industrial facilities often deal with power factor issues that can affect energy efficiency and lead to penalties from utility companies. Electricians may install power factor correction equipment to mitigate these issues.

Arc Flash Analysis and Safety

Electricians perform arc flash analysis to assess the potential hazards of electrical systems and implement safety measures to protect workers from arc flash incidents.
